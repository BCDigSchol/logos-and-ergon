Taras was colonized by Spartans, and the founder was a Spartiate named Phalanthus. An oracle came to him from Delphi as he was about to set out to found the colony, which said that when he saw rain falling from a clear sky, at that time he would found his city and land. [7] At first he did not examine this oracle himself, nor did he talk with any of the interpreters of prophecies, but he sailed to Italy with his ships. Although he defeated the barbarians there, he was not able to seize any of their cities nor take possession of the land. But then he remembered the oracle, and he thought that what the god had prophesied was impossible, since it would never rain under a clear and cloudless sky. Then, as he was losing hope, his wife, who had accompanied him from home, among other kindnesses, placed her husband’s head in her lap. … And because of her affection for him, she began to weep, seeing her husband’s plans come to naught. [8] As her tears fell profusely and got his head wet, he understood the prophecy, since his wife’s name was Aithra.<sup><a href="#note-1">1</a></sup> On that very night, he seized Taras – the largest and most prosperous of the cities on that sea – from the barbarians.
