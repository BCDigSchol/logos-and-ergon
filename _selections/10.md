A Samian ship, owned by Kolaeus, was sailing to Egypt and put in at the island of Platea.<sup><a href="#note-1">1</a></sup> [2] Then, eager to get to Egypt, they launched their ship and sailed away, but were driven off course by an east wind, which did not stop blowing until they passed through the Pillars of Heracles<sup><a href="#note-2">2</a></sup> and arrived at Tartessus, with a divine escort. [3] This was an entirely untapped market at this time, with the result that when they returned home, they made the largest profit from their cargo of any Greek I know of with certainty, apart from Sostratus, son of Laodamas, from Aegina; no one can compete with him. [4] The Samians took six talents as a tithe of their profits and had a bronze krater made in the Argive style, with rows of griffin heads around the rim. They dedicated it in the temple of Hera, putting it on a pedestal consisting of three kneeling bronze figures, each seven cubits high.<sup><a href="#note-3">3</a></sup>
