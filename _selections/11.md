preface: "Amphipolis, on the coast of the north Aegean, had been founded as a colony by Athens in 438 BCE; the oikist was an Athenian named Hagnon. During the Peloponnesian War, Amphipolis was captured by the Spartan general Brasidas in 424. When Brasidas was killed in battle nearby in 422, the people of Amphipolis declared that he, not Hagnon, was their oikist."
---

All the allies, attending in full armor, gave Brasidas a public funeral, burying him in front of the agora. And for the future, the people of Amphipolis enclosed his tomb within a sacred boundary, and they offer sacrifice to him as a hero and honor him with athletic competitions and annual sacrifices. They declared him to be the founder of the colony, tearing down the monuments of Hagnon, and if any memorial of Hagnon’s foundation survived, they obliterated it. They did this because they considered Brasidas their savior, and since at the time they were cultivating their alliance with Sparta due to fear of Athens, they could no longer enjoyably and advantageously honor Hagnon, being now enemies of Athens.
