\tJust as my father and yours, great fool Perses,\n
\toften sailed here in ships, seeking a good life.\n
635\tHe once came here, finishing a long sea voyage,\n
\tLeaving Aeolian Kyme in a black ship,\n
\tFleeing not possessions and wealth and prosperity,\n
\tBut evil poverty, which Zeus gives to men.\n
\tHe settled here near Mt. Helicon, in a miserable village,\n
640\tAscra, which is bad in winter, troublesome in summer, and good never.
