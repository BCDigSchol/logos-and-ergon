Two cities located between Catana and Syracuse, namely Naxos and Megara, have vanished.<a href="#note-1">1</a></sup>

… Ephorus says that these were the first Greek cities founded in Sicily, in the tenth generation after the Trojan War. Earlier, he says, the Greeks feared Etruscan piracy and the savagery of the barbarians there so much that they did not sail to Sicily for the purpose of trade. But Theocles of Athens, who had been blown off course to Sicily by the winds, recognized both the weakness of the people and the quality of the land there. When he returned home, he could not persuade the Athenians to colonize Sicily, so he gathered many men from Chalcis in Euboea, along with other Ionians and even some Dorians, most of whom were from Megara, and sailed with them to Sicily. The Chalcidians founded Naxos and the Dorians founded Megara, which had earlier been called Hybla.
