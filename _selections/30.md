108. The Budini are a large and populous nation, who almost all have grey eyes and red hair. In their territory, a city made of wood has been built, whose name is Gelonus. The wall enclosing the city is nearly four miles long on each side, very high and made entirely of wood. The houses and temples in it are also wooden. [2] There are shrines there dedicated to Greek gods and built in Greek style, with wooden statues and altars and temples. They honor Dionysus with a festival and Bacchic rituals every two years. For the Gelonians were originally Greeks, who were forced out of the trading ports and now live among the Budini. They speak a language that is partly Greek and partly Scythian.

109. The Budini do not speak the same language as the Gelonians, nor is their way of life the same. The Budini are indigenous nomads, who are the only ones in that region to eat pine cones, whereas the Gelonians work the land, eat grain, and plant fruit trees. Neither their appearance nor coloring are similar. Still, the Budini are called Gelonians by other Greeks, even though this is incorrect.
