If it were proposed for discussion that each nation should choose which customs, out of all customs, are the best, each of them, after careful consideration, would choose their own. To that extent each nation thinks its own customs are by far the best. [2] … It is possible to provide much other evidence that every nation thinks in this way about its own customs, but I will mention just this one. [3] When Darius was king,<sup><a href="#note-1">1</a></sup> he summoned some of the Greeks who were at his court and asked them how much money he would have to pay them before they would be willing to eat the bodies of their deceased fathers. They said that they would never do this, not for any amount of money. [4] Then Darius summoned the Indians who are called the Callatiae, whose custom is to eat their parents. While the Greeks were present and learning what was said through a translator, he asked them for how much money they would burn the bodies of their deceased fathers with fire. They cried out and asked him not to say such terrible things. That’s how strong these customs are, and so it seems to me that Pindar was correct when he wrote in his poems that “custom is the king of all.”
