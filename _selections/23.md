preface: "Pindar wrote this ode to celebrate the athletic victory of Arcesilas, the king of the Greek colony of Cyrene in North Africa, in the chariot race at the Pythian Games, which were celebrated at Delphi. As a way of praising Arcesilas, Pindar describes the original foundation of Cyrene by Arcesilas’ ancestor Battus and the honors Battus received from the city."

\tAristoteles<sup><a href="#note-1">1</a></sup> brought men there<sup><a href="#note-2">2</a></sup> in swift ships,\n
\twhen he opened up the deep way through the sea.\n
\tHe established greater groves for the gods,\n
90\tand built a flat and level roadway for processions\n
\tthat honor Apollo and protect mortals\n
\tas the gravel path resounds with hoofbeats.\n
\tAnd there on the edge of the agora, he lies apart in death.\n
\tHe dwelled happily among men while alive,\n
95\tand now is a hero worshipped by the people.\n
\tThe other sacred kings\n
\twhose lot is death\n
\tlie separately in front of the palace,\n
\tand they hear in their minds beneath the earth\n
100\tof the great virtue sprinkled with soft dew\n
\tin the outpourings of songs,\n
\tof their good fortune and the glory,\n
\tjustly shared, of their son Arcesilas.
