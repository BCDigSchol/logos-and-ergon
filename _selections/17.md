Massilia is a colony of the Phocaeans, and is situated on a rocky site. … On a headland is located the temple of Artemis of Ephesus and also the temple of Apollo Delphinios, which is shared by all Ionians. The temple of Artemis, by contrast, is dedicated solely to Artemis of Ephesus. The story goes that when the Phocaeans were about to leave their homeland to found the colony, they received an oracle that said they should use a guide for their voyage given to them by Artemis of Ephesus. So some of them stopped in Ephesus to find out how they could acquire the guide from the goddess. Now, Aristarche, one of the most respected women in Ephesus, had a dream in which the goddess came to her and told her to sail away with the Phocaeans and bring with her  statue of the goddess. Thus, when the colony was established, they founded a temple and gave Aristarche the exceptional honor of making her the priestess. Among Massilia’s colonies generally they worship this goddess especially, and they depict the goddess in a particular Ephesian form called the xoanon, and they preserve all the other customs just as in the mother-city.
