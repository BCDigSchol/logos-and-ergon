\tTlepolemus, son of Heracles, a great and noble man,\n
\tled out from Rhodes nine ships of lordly Rhodians,\n
655\twho dwell in Rhodes, which is divided into three parts,\n
\tLindus and Ialysus and shining Camirus.\n
\tTlepolemus, famed for the spear, led them,\n
\twhom Astyochia bore to forceful Heracles,\n
\twhom he led away from Ephyra<a href="#note-1">1</a></sup> and the river Selleis,\n
660\tafter sacking many cities of men cherished by Zeus.\n
\tWhen Tlepolemus had grown up in the well-built halls,\n
\the killed his father’s beloved uncle,\n
\tLicymnius, offspring of Ares, who was growing old.\n
\tSwiftly he built ships and gathered a large army,\n
665\tand went across the sea in flight, since the other\n
\tsons and grandsons of Heracles threatened him.\n
\tHe came to Rhodes in his wanderings, suffering grievously,\n
\tAnd settled there in three divisions according to tribe.\n
\tThey were beloved by Zeus who rules gods and men,\n
670\tAnd the son of Cronus poured out divine wealth upon them.
