Next along the coast is Neapolis, a colony of Cumae. Later the Chalcidians sent new colonists, along with some people from Pithecusae and Athenians, with the result that it was called Neapolis (“New City”). A monument of Parthenope, one of the Sirens, can be seen there, and athletic contests are celebrated in her honor, as directed by an oracle. Later, when the Neapolitans were divided into factions, they admitted some of the Campanians as fellow citizens, and they were forced to treat their most hated enemies as their closest friends, since they had made their proper friends hostile. The names of the magistrates reveal this, since at first they are entirely Greek, but then become a mixture of Greek and Campanian. Many traces of Greek culture are preserved here, such as gymnasia, training-grounds for young men, phratries,<a href="#note-1">1</a></sup> and Greek names of things, although the people are now Roman citizens.
