Emporion is a colony of Massilia. … This whole coast is fertile and has good harbors. There is also a village called Rhode, which some say was established by Emporion and others by Rhodes. In both Rhode and Emporion, they worship Artemis of Ephesus, and I will explain the reason for that in my account of Massilia. The Emporitans first settled an offshore island, which is now called the Old City, but now they live on the mainland. The city is in two parts divided by a wall, because in the past the neighboring (non-Greek) Indicetans wanted to share a city wall with the Greeks for safety, although they governed themselves separately. Over time, they unified into a single citizen body and used a mixture of Greek and barbarian customs. This sort of thing has happened in many other cases.
