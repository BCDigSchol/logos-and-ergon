Historians disagree about why Battus consulted the oracle. … Some say that it was because of political strife in the city. Menecles of Barca says that the story about political strife seems more trustworthy, but the story about his stammering voice seems more mythical. He says that the citizens in Thera were at loggerheads with each other, and Battus was the leader of one of the factions. When the factions were fighting fiercely, Battus’ faction was driven out of the city and fled from its territory. Despairing of effecting a return to their homeland, they made plans about founding a colony. Battus went to Delphi and asked whether they should continue to fight the other side or found a colony. The god answered, "Battus, the first thing you ask about is bad and the second one is good. Go, leave behind the salt-washed land,<sup><a href="#note-1">1</a></sup> since the eastern continent is better. First cast aside trickery, and persuade your people by being persuasive. Be pious towards the harsh land, which you hate unlawfully. Whatever a man will accomplish, a similar end will come for him."
