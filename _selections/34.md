There is another story told throughout Greece, which is that Xerxes sent a herald to Argos before he started his expedition against Greece. [2] It is said that the messenger went there and said, “Argives, King Xerxes says to you, ‘We believe that the man Perses, from whom we Persians are descended, was the son of Perseus the son of Danae, and the son of Andromeda the daughter of Cepheus. This, we are your descendants. It is not reasonable for us to march against our ancestors, nor that you should become our enemies by helping others against us. Rather, you should sit still and stay neutral. If everything goes according to my desires, I will hold no one in higher esteem than you.’” Hearing this, the Argives supposedly made a big deal of this offer, and although they neither promised nor asked for anything immediately. Later, however, the Greeks asked for their help, they asked that the Spartans hand over leadership to them. They knew that the Spartans would refuse, and used this as their excuse to remain neutral.
