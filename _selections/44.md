When Leucippus of Sparta inquired where it was fated for him and his people to settle, the god answered that he should sail to Italy and settle where, after they landed by ship, they would stay for a day and a night. The expedition landed near Callipolis, a harbor belonging to the Tarantines. Leucippus, pleased with the nature of this site, persuaded the Tarantines to allow them to encamp there for a day and a night. After more days passed, the Tarantines asked them to depart, but Leucippus paid no attention, saying that they had agreed to give him the land for day and night, and as long as either one of these existed, he would not give up the land. The Tarantines, realizing that they had been led astray, allowed them to stay.
