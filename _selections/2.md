\tThere is a fertile island stretching outside of the harbor,\n
\tneither near the land of the Cyclopes nor far away,\n
\tand wooded. Plentiful wild goats live there,\n
\tfor there is no footstep of men to ward them off,\n
120\tnor do hunters go there, who would suffer pain\n
\tpursuing them across the wooded mountain peaks.\n
\tNor is it occupied by flocks or plowland,\n
\tbut bereft of men, it remains unsown and unplowed\n
\tall the time, and nourishes the bleating goats.\n
125\tFor there are no red-sided ships among the Cyclopes,\n
\tnor ship-building men, who would toil building\n
\twell-benched ships, which would complete the journey\n
\tto every city of men, just as men generally\n
\tcross the sea to visit each other in ships.\n
130\tThey would make this a well-tilled island.\n
\tIt is not a bad place at all, bearing all crops in season.\n
\tOn it there are soft well-watered meadows by the shore\n
\tof the grey sea, and there are boundless grape-vines.\n
\tThe plow-land is deep, and they would harvest a rich crop\n
135\tin every season, since the soil underneath is rich.\n
\tIt has a good harbor, where there is no need of a stern-cable,\n
\tNor to throw an anchor or fasten ropes, but the sailor\n
\tpulling his ship onto land can wait for a time, until\n
\this spirit urges him on and the winds blow.\n
140\tShining water flows at the head of the harbor,\n
\ta spring from a cave, and poplar trees grow around it.
