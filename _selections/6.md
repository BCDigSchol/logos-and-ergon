preface: "This inscription was carved by Greek mercenaries into the leg of a colossal statue of the pharaoh Ramses II at a temple called Abu Simbel, in the far south of Egypt. The mercenaries were in the service of an Egyptian king, Psammetichus II (595-589 BCE), who was campaigning far up the Nile into Ethiopia. Other mercenaries also carved their names and hometowns, which included Teos, Colophon, and Ialysus."

When King Psammetichus came to Elephantine, those who sailed with Psammetichus son of Theocles wrote this. They went above Kerkis as far as the river allowed. Potasimto commanded those of foreign speech, and Amasis commanded the Egyptians. Archon son of Amoebichus wrote this, along with Pelekus son of Oudamus.
