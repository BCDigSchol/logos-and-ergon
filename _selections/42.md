Some men from Cnidus and Rhodes, who were suffering from the harsh treatment of the kings in Asia, decided to send out a colony. [2] They chose as their leader Pentathlus of Cnidus, who traced his ancestry to Hippotes, a descendent of Heracles. In the fiftieth Olympiad [580 BCE], Pentathlus and his men sailed to Sicily, specifically to the region around Lilybaeum. There they found the people of Segesta and Selinus<sup><a href="#note-1">1</a></sup> at war with each other. [3] They were persuaded by Selinus to ally with them, but they lost many men in battle, including Pentathlus. The survivors decided to return home, since the Selinuntines had been defeated. They chose as their new leaders Gorgus, Thestor, and Epithersides, who were relatives of Pentathlus, and sailed off through the Tyrrhenian Sea. [4] But when they stopped at Lipara and received a friendly welcome, by general agreement they were persuaded to settle on Lipara with the locals.
