The Egyptian priests agree on this: the Egyptians were the first of all people to discover the year, and to divide it into twelve parts according to the seasons. They say that they discovered this from the stars. It seems to me that they organize the calendar more wisely than the Greeks, inasmuch as the Greeks add an extra month<sup><a href="#note-1">1</a></sup> every other year to stay in line with the seasons. But because the Egyptians divide the year into twelve months of thirty days each, and then add five extra days each year, the cycle of the seasons comes around to the same point every year. [2] The priests also say that the Egyptians first discovered the names of the twelve gods and that the Greeks learned this from them, and also that Egyptians were the first to build altars, statues, and temples for the gods and to sculpt statues of them in stone. Most of this the priests proved with evidence.
