preface: "Greeks debated whether the Macedonians counted as Greeks or not. Herodotus tells a story about Alexander I of Macedon (not Alexander the Great, but his ancestor, who ruled c. 498-454) competing at the Olympic Games, which were restricted to Greeks. This story not only suggests that the kings of Macedonia, at least, were Greeks, but also the criterion by which they made that claim."

The descendants of Perdiccas<sup><a href="#note-1">1</a></sup> were Greeks, as I happen to know that they say themselves, and I will prove later in my Histories that they are Greeks. But moreover, the men who manage the contest at Olympia recognized that they were Greeks in the following way. [2] When Alexander wanted to compete and went down to Olympia, the Greeks who were going to compete against him objected, saying that this was not a contest of barbarians but of Greeks. When Alexander showed that he was descended from people from Argos, they judged that he was a Greek, and he ran the one-stadion<sup><a href="#note-2">2</a></sup> race and tied for first place.
