The Phocaeans were the first Greeks to make long sea-voyages, and the first to discover the Adriatic and Tyrrhenian seas, as well as the lands of Iberia and Tartessus. [2] They made these voyages not with merchant ships but with 50-oared warships. Arriving in Tartessus, they made friends with the king of Tartessus, whose name was Arganthonius. He ruled Tartessus for 80 years, and lived for 120. [3] The Phocaeans became so friendly with him that he offered to let them leave Ionia and settle in his land, wherever they wanted. When they refused, but he learned that Persian power was growing near them, he gave them money to build a wall around the city. [4] He gave generously, since the circuit of the wall is not short, and is built entirely of large and well-fitted stones.
